Danish Butter Cookies

A timeless classic recognized worldwide by its iconic blue tin, Danish butter cookies are crisp, rich, and deeply satisfying. Made with just a few quality ingredients — butter, flour, sugar, and vanilla — they come in beautifully piped shapes and have been a beloved treat across Scandinavia and beyond for generations.

Cookie Denmark Sweet Easy

The Blue Tin

The iconic Royal Dansk blue tin has taken on a life of its own — repurposed for generations as a sewing kit, storage box, or keepsake. It's become a cultural symbol almost as famous as the cookies inside.

The Recipe

Yield 24–36 cookies
Prep Time 15–20 minutes
Baking Time 10–12 minutes
Total Time 25–32 minutes

What you'll need

  • Parchment paper
  • Good quality baking sheets
  • Cookie press or piping bag with star tip

Ingredients

  • 225g (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 100g (½ cup) granulated sugar
  • 240g (2 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp salt (if using unsalted butter)
  • Granulated sugar, for dusting (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat & prepare Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F) and line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats.
  2. Cream butter and sugar In a mixing bowl, beat softened butter and sugar together until the mixture becomes light and fluffy, about 3–4 minutes using a hand or stand mixer.
  3. Add vanilla Blend in the vanilla extract thoroughly. Add salt if using unsalted butter.
  4. Incorporate flour Gradually add flour to the butter mixture, stirring until a dough forms. If needed, knead gently by hand to ensure all flour is fully incorporated.
  5. Shape the cookies Use a cookie press, a piping bag fitted with a star tip, or roll dough into small balls and flatten gently with a fork to create the classic ridged pattern.
  6. Add topping Optionally sprinkle a pinch of granulated sugar over each cookie before baking.
  7. Bake Bake for 10–12 minutes until the edges turn lightly golden. The centers may look soft — they will firm up as they cool.
  8. Cool Let cookies rest on the baking sheet for 2–3 minutes, then transfer to a wire cooling rack until completely cooled.

Tips & Notes

The quality of butter will significantly impact the final flavor — use a premium European-style butter for the best results. These cookies keep well in an airtight tin for up to two weeks.
